Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our Florida sign services.

Yes, all of our Florida exterior signs are engineered to meet Florida Building Code requirements for wind resistance. Our signs are designed and tested to withstand high-velocity hurricane zone (HVHZ) wind loads where required, often rated up to 185 MPH. We use reinforced mounting systems, impact-resistant materials, and provide structural engineering documentation for permit approval.
We serve South Florida and the Treasure Coast from our Stuart office. Our primary service areas include Martin County (Stuart, Palm City, Jensen Beach), St. Lucie County (Port St. Lucie, Fort Pierce), Indian River County (Vero Beach, Sebastian), Palm Beach County (West Palm Beach, Boca Raton, Jupiter), Broward County (Fort Lauderdale), and Miami-Dade County.
Florida sign costs vary by type, size, and engineering requirements. Hurricane-rated channel letters typically range from $4,000-$18,000+, monument signs from $6,000-$30,000+, pylon signs from $12,000-$60,000+, and vehicle wraps from $2,500-$5,000+. Florida signs require additional engineering for wind loads, which is included in our quotes. We provide free on-site consultations.
Yes, we manage all Florida sign permitting as part of our turnkey service. This includes permit applications, structural engineering documents required for Florida Building Code compliance, and coordination with municipal inspectors. We have experience with Martin County, Palm Beach County, St. Lucie County, and South Florida municipality requirements.
Florida timelines include time for engineering and permit approval. Simple signs like banners take 1-2 weeks. Channel letters typically take 6-8 weeks including design, engineering, permitting, and fabrication. Monument signs and pylons take 10-14 weeks due to foundation work, engineering reviews, and more complex permitting. We provide detailed timelines during your consultation.
